IRISH COFFEE

Ingredient Unit Amount
Irish Whiskey ML 45
Coffee U 1
Cream ML 50
Sugar Syrup Homemade ML 15
Coffe Beans U 3

Method: Built

Glass: Irish Glass

International Day: 25 JAN

PREPARATION

Warm black coffee is poured into a preheated Irish coffee glass. Whiskey and at least one teaspoon of sugar is added and stirred until dissolved. Fresh thick chilled cream is carefully poured over the back of a spoon held just above the surface of the coffee. The layer of cream will float on the coffee without mixing. Plain sugar can be replaced with sugar syrup.
Garnish: 3 coffee beans on top
IF REQUESTED: ALGARVIAN COFFEE substitute with medronho.
IF REQUESTED: SCOTCH COFFEE substitute with scotch whisky.
IF REQUESTED: ITALIAN COFFEE substitute with grappa.
IF REQUESTED: BAILEYS COFFEE substitute with baileys

STORYTELLING

Irish Coffee was created in 1942 by chef Joe Sheridan at Foynes Airbase in Ireland. After a group of cold, tired passengers returned due to bad weather, he added Irish whiskey and cream to their coffee to warm them up. A passenger called it "Irish Coffee," and the drink became a hit. It was later popularized in the U.S. by bartender Jack Koeppler at the St. Regis Hotel in San Francisco in the 1950s. Today, it's a beloved classic worldwide.

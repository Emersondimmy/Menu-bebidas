STORYTELLING

Sangria originated in Spain, with its roots dating back to the 18th century. The drink was originally a way to make inexpensive wine more flavorful by adding fruits, spices, and sometimes spirits like brandy. The name "sangria" comes from the Spanish word *sangre*, meaning "blood," due to its deep red color. While its exact origin is unclear, sangria has long been associated with Spanish and Portuguese culture, particularly as a refreshing drink enjoyed during warm weather and festive occasions.
